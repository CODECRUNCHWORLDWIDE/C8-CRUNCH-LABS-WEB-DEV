1. **[Challenge 1 — Build a to-do app, no framework](./challenge-01-build-a-todo-app-no-framework.md)** — the warm-up version of this week's mini-project, scoped down to 140 minutes. (~140 min)

Challenges are optional but transformational. Skip them and you will know JavaScript well. Do them and the apps you use every day will stop feeling like a black box.

## How challenges differ from exercises

- **Exercises** are small drills with a known answer. You can grade yourself.
- **Challenges** are larger problems that require judgment. There is no single right answer — there are better and worse code choices, and your reasoning is part of the work.
- **Mini-projects** are the largest synthesis. They take a full day or more and ship something you would put in your portfolio.

You can skip a challenge and still pass Week 4. But challenges are where the language stops feeling like a textbook and starts feeling like a real material. Pick at least one.

## Workflow

- Block out a single 2-hour focused session per challenge.
- Disable distractions. Open the source material, your editor, and DevTools.
- Treat it like a real brief — except the reviewer is your future self in Week 12, looking back at how you handled state the first time.
