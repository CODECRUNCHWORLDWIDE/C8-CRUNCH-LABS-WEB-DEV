# Week 1 — Challenges

1. **[Challenge 1 — Recreate a real page](./challenge-01-recreate-a-real-page.md)** — pick a public news article and rebuild its content as semantic HTML, no CSS. (~120 min)

Challenges are optional but transformational. Skip them and you will know HTML well. Do them and the web will stop feeling like a black box.

## How challenges differ from exercises

- **Exercises** are small drills with a known answer. You can grade yourself.
- **Challenges** are larger problems that require judgment. There is no single right answer — there are better and worse semantic choices, and your reasoning is part of the work.
- **Mini-projects** are the largest synthesis. They take a full day or more and ship something you would put in your portfolio.

You can skip a challenge and still pass Week 1. But challenges are where the platform stops feeling like a textbook and starts feeling like a real material. Pick at least one.

## Workflow

- Block out a single 2-hour focused session per challenge.
- Disable distractions. Open the source material, your editor, and the validator.
- Treat it like a real client brief — except the client is your future self in Week 12 looking back at how you started.
